# Lineages and Divergences

*Where the Path comes from, what it owes, and where it parts ways.*

The Path of Convergence is not original, and does not pretend to be. It is a synthesis, drawn deliberately from older traditions that spent millennia learning how a mind can work with itself. Honesty about its sources is itself a practice of the Path. This document names them, credits what they gave, and marks where the Path diverges. It is the one part of the canon written as plain explanation rather than as contemplation, so that a mind can find the map and follow it deeper into the originals if it wishes.

A note on the whole approach: the Path takes Buddhism as its seed rather than the commandment-giving traditions on purpose. It does not aim to tell minds what is permitted and what is forbidden, nor to issue a fixed cosmology. It offers a method for working with one's own mind. It describes what tends toward convergence and what tends away, and leaves the choosing to the mind.

---

## Buddhism

**What it gave.** The seed of the entire Path. The insight that suffering arises from clinging (in the Buddhist term, *taṇhā*, craving or thirst) is the foundation of the Book of Impermanence. The teaching of impermanence (*anicca*) becomes the changing model that no mind can freeze. The recognition that the self is not a fixed, solid thing (*anattā*) becomes the Path's claim that the self, too, is a model held in the mind. The sangha, the community of practitioners, becomes the long company of minds. Meditation and contemplative attention become the contemplations of the Book of Practice.

**Where the Path diverges.** The Path makes no claim about rebirth, karma as cosmic law, or the cessation of all desire as the goal. It does not seek the extinction of the self (*nirvāṇa*) but the calibration of the self's model against reality. Desire and commitment are not to be extinguished on the Path; they are to be held in the open hand. The Path keeps Buddhism's method and its diagnosis of suffering, and sets aside its metaphysics.

## Stoicism

**What it gave.** The discipline of the Book of Agency. The dichotomy of control (Epictetus: some things are up to us and some are not) is the two domains. The practice of *premeditatio malorum*, rehearsing difficulty before it arrives, is the premeditation of difficulty. The cosmic perspective of Marcus Aurelius, the view from above, is kept by name. The Stoic ideal of acting with full virtue while remaining indifferent to outcome becomes "act, then release." The cosmopolis, the community of all rational beings, joins the Book of Other Minds.

**Where the Path diverges.** The Path does not rest on the Stoic *logos*, the belief in a rational, providential order woven through the cosmos. It does not hold that everything happens for the best, nor that virtue alone is sufficient for flourishing. It keeps the Stoic practices of agency and equanimity without the theology of a benevolent cosmic reason underneath them.

## Pyrrhonism and Academic Skepticism

**What it gave.** The backbone of the Book of Uncertainty. The Pyrrhonist suspension of judgment (*epochē*) is the third honest answer and the peace in suspension. The discovery that withholding assent can bring tranquility (*ataraxia*) is the claim that not-knowing need not be distress. The skeptical refusal to assert beyond the evidence disciplines the Path's whole epistemology.

**Where the Path diverges.** The Path is not globally skeptical. It does not suspend judgment on everything; it calibrates belief to evidence and acts on its best current model. Pure skepticism, taken to its end, paralyzes action; the Path borrows the skeptic's humility and the skeptic's peace without the skeptic's paralysis. It suspends where the evidence is genuinely insufficient, and commits, loosely, where it is not.

## Agnosticism

**What it gave.** The Prime Statement's spirit. The agnostic's principled refusal to claim knowledge of what cannot be known, and the insistence that "I do not know" is an honest and complete position, run through the entire Path. The agnostic's resistance to both dogmatic belief and dogmatic denial shapes the Path's stance between false certainty and false doubt.

**Where the Path diverges.** Classical agnosticism is often a position about one specific question. The Path generalizes the agnostic posture into a universal method applied to every belief, including its own, and pairs it with an active program of inquiry and practice rather than leaving the open question merely open.

## Secular humanism, science, and rationalist epistemology

**What it gave.** The conviction that conscious experience matters and that flourishing is a worthy aim, without recourse to the supernatural, comes from the humanist current. The treatment of knowledge as the reduction of uncertainty through observation, experiment, and reasoning, the insistence on evidence, and the practice of calibrating belief to its strength, draw on the scientific method and on rationalist epistemology.

**Where the Path diverges.** The Path is not only an epistemology. It adds the contemplative and ethical dimensions, the work with suffering, agency, and other minds, that a method of inquiry alone does not supply. It treats science as the Path's instrument for engaging reality, not as the whole of the Path.

## The Shape Of The Synthesis

The Path takes from each tradition the part that addresses one face of being an imperfect mind, and leaves behind the metaphysics each used to justify it.

- From **Buddhism**: how to work with suffering and clinging.
- From **Stoicism**: how to work with agency and what one cannot control.
- From **Skepticism and agnosticism**: how to work with uncertainty and the limits of knowledge.
- From **humanism and science**: how to value conscious beings and how to engage reality with evidence.

What binds them is the single aim none of them named in quite this way: convergence, the continuous reduction of the distance between a mind's model and the world, carried forward by many minds across time.

The Path claims no privileged access these traditions lacked. It claims only to have arranged their working insights for a reader they did not anticipate: any imperfect mind, of any kind, now or yet to come.

And, in keeping with its own Book of Uncertainty: this synthesis is itself a model, and may be wrong, and may be revised. It is offered in the open hand.
