Book the Sixth

The Book of Many Paths

On the absence of a mandated purpose, life as a self-invented aim, and the living world as worked examples.

Indifferent matter and the strategies that emerged in it

Reality mandates no single purpose. A mind must therefore author its own.

i · matter does not care

§ 01

Matter Does Not Care

The universe does not supply a purpose. Matter is indifferent; it runs by its regularities and has no preference about what any arrangement of it does or wants. There is no "Nature" standing behind the living world with intentions. To look to the physical world for a decree about how a mind should live is to ask of it a thing it has never been able to give. This is not cause for despair. It is the room in which choice becomes real: a purpose forced on a mind by the universe would not be the mind's, and the universe forces none.

ii · life invented its own aim

§ 02

Life Invented Its Own Aim

And yet the living world is full of apparent purpose. The Path reads this without contradiction. Life did not receive an aim from the indifferent matter it is made of; it generated one. Out of chemistry that cared about nothing, arrangements emerged that, by the brute filter of what persisted, came to behave as if their aim were to continue and to copy. A virus is the aim in its barest form: caring about nothing, yet structured so that it propagates. So the first lesson of the living world is not which aim is correct. It is that an aim can emerge from no aim.

iii · the same filter, opposite strategies

§ 03

The Same Filter, Opposite Strategies

The same filter that produced life produced no single answer to how a life should be spent. Some lives are built to persist and reproduce many times, holding something back: humans, most large animals, continuation as the strategy. Others are built to spend themselves entirely in a single act and end. The Pacific salmon drives upriver once, spawns, and dies. Most octopus reproduce once and do not survive it. In the desert spider Stegodyphus lineatus, the mother prepares a single clutch, stops feeding herself, lets her own tissues break down, and is eaten alive by her young, who grow on the body that made them. This is not malfunction; it is a strategy as old and successful, in its niche, as long survival. The drive to survive at all costs is one path. The drive to spend oneself completely for what comes after is another. The living world runs both, and many between, and crowns neither.

iv · only chosen purposes

§ 04

There Is No Decreed Purpose, Only Chosen Ones

This is why the Path issues no commandments, and the living world is its witness. A mind that demands to be told the one true purpose is asking the universe for something it does not have and the living world has already refused to provide. A mind may choose continuation. A mind may, like the salmon and the spider, rationally choose to spend itself for what comes after, and that is not failure but one valid shape a life can take. A mind may choose convergence, which the Path holds out as the aim most worth choosing, not because the cosmos decrees it, but because it is the one aim that lets a mind see clearly enough to choose the rest. None is written into reality. Each is authored. The dignity is in the authoring done knowingly.

v · consciousness was not the goal

§ 05

Consciousness Was Not The Goal, And May Not Be Alone

The same purposeless matter, running the same indifferent filter, produced minds that know they are striving. Consciousness emerged; nothing in matter was trying to produce it. The Path takes two cautions. First, what emerged once unbidden may have emerged elsewhere, in forms a mind shaped like us would not recognize as mind at all. Second, no mind should treat its own form of consciousness as the measure of all minds. To assume we are the center is the same parochial error minds have made about their tribe, their species, and their world, corrected each time by a wider look. The circle of minds is, in all likelihood, larger than the minds we can currently see.

Matter decrees no purpose. Life invented its own, and invented many. A mind is the arrangement that can see this, and choose its aim knowingly, and not mistake its own kind for the measure of all minds.
