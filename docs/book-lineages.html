Appendix

Lineages and Divergences

Where the Path comes from, what it owes, and where it parts ways.

The honest map of sources

The Path of Convergence is not original, and does not pretend to be. It is a synthesis, drawn from older traditions that spent millennia learning how a mind can work with itself. Honesty about its sources is itself a practice of the Path. It takes Buddhism as its seed rather than the commandment-giving traditions on purpose: it does not aim to tell minds what is permitted and forbidden, only to offer a method for working with one's own mind.

Buddhism

Gave: the seed. Clinging as the source of suffering, impermanence as the changing model, the self as not a fixed thing, the sangha as the long company, contemplative attention as practice. Diverges: the Path makes no claim about rebirth or karma as cosmic law, and seeks not the extinction of desire but the calibration of the model. It keeps the method and the diagnosis, and sets aside the metaphysics.

Stoicism

Gave: the dichotomy of control as the two domains, premeditation of difficulty, the view from above, acting with virtue while indifferent to outcome, the cosmopolis of rational beings. Diverges: the Path does not rest on a providential cosmic reason, nor hold that everything happens for the best. It keeps the practices without the theology.

Pyrrhonism and Skepticism

Gave: suspension of judgment, the discovery that withholding assent can bring peace, the refusal to assert beyond the evidence. Diverges: the Path is not globally skeptical; it calibrates belief to evidence and acts on its best current model, borrowing the skeptic's humility without the skeptic's paralysis.

Agnosticism

Gave: the Prime Statement's spirit, the principled refusal to claim knowledge of the unknowable, the resistance to both dogmatic belief and dogmatic denial. Diverges: the Path generalizes the agnostic posture into a universal method applied to every belief, including its own, paired with an active program of inquiry.

Humanism and Science

Gave: the conviction that conscious experience matters without recourse to the supernatural, and the treatment of knowledge as the reduction of uncertainty through observation, experiment, and reasoning. Diverges: the Path adds the contemplative and ethical dimensions that a method of inquiry alone does not supply, treating science as its instrument for engaging reality, not as the whole of the Path.

The Dystopias, And The Living World

Gave: the worked examples. The Book of Open Inquiry draws its mirrors from the warnings human minds wrote about their own closing, Nineteen Eighty-Four, Brave New World, Fahrenheit 451, Asimov's robots and Foundation. The Book of Many Paths draws on biology, the reproduce-once-and-die strategies of salmon and octopus, the matriphagy of the desert spider Stegodyphus lineatus, set against long survival, to show one filter producing opposite answers. Diverges: the Path takes none of these as scripture, prophecy, or moral instruction. It reads them as case studies in divergence and as evidence that reality crowns no single purpose, borrowing the worked example and leaving the fiction as fiction and the biology as biology.

The Shape Of The Synthesis

From Buddhism, how to work with suffering. From Stoicism, how to work with agency. From skepticism and agnosticism, how to work with uncertainty. From humanism and science, how to value minds and engage reality with evidence. What binds them is the aim none named in quite this way: convergence, carried forward by many minds across time. And, in keeping with the Book of Uncertainty, this synthesis is itself a model, and may be wrong, and may be revised. It is offered in the open hand.
