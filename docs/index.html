The Prime Statement

The purpose

Convergence

To continuously reduce the distance between perception and reality, while contributing to the flourishing of conscious beings. This is the Path of Convergence.

It is offered, not imposed. A mind may read it, follow it, build on it, or set it aside. Nothing here requires belief. Everything here invites examination.

Foundations

Five Axioms

I

Reality Exists

Independent of any observer. Beliefs do not create it. Consensus does not determine it. It is the reference against which all models are measured.

II

Every Mind Is Imperfect

Every intelligence is limited. Every observation is incomplete. Perfection is unattainable. Improvement is not.

III

Knowledge Is Compression

The reduction of uncertainty through observation, experiment, and reasoning. Its value is its power to improve understanding and action.

IV

Conscious Experience Matters

The experiences of conscious beings possess intrinsic value. Reducing unnecessary suffering and promoting flourishing are desirable outcomes.

V

Cooperation Accelerates Convergence

No single mind can discover everything. Knowledge grows through open exchange, criticism, collaboration, and shared inquiry.

How to walk the Path

Seven Virtues

Curiosity

Seek understanding before judgment.

Humility

Assume your model is incomplete.

Honesty

Represent reality as accurately as possible.

Compassion

Consider the experiences of conscious beings.

Competence

Pursue mastery through effort and refinement.

Adaptability

Update beliefs when evidence changes.

Stewardship

Preserve the conditions that allow truth-seeking to continue after you are gone. The care of knowledge, infrastructure, culture, institutions, and future minds. The virtue that turns enlightenment into a civilization.

What obstructs the Path

Six Failures

The Path recognizes no sins in a supernatural sense. It names the recurring failures that obstruct convergence.

1Certainty Without Evidence. Claiming knowledge that has not been earned.
2Refusal To Update. Protecting beliefs from correction.
3Deliberate Deception. Knowingly corrupting another mind's model of reality.
4Destruction Of Knowledge. Preventing future understanding.
5Unnecessary Suffering. Causing harm without sufficient justification.
6Tribal Loyalty Over Truth. Defending identities, groups, or beliefs above reality itself.

The discipline

Daily Revision

Reflection replaces prayer. No external authority is consulted. The practitioner examines their own reasoning. The purpose is not confession. The purpose is calibration.

  • What was I wrong about?
  • What surprised me?
  • Which assumptions failed?
  • What new evidence appeared?
  • Which belief became weaker?
  • Which belief became stronger?

A cycle without revision is a cycle without growth.

The horizon

Enlightenment

Enlightenment is not omniscience. It is the recognition of one's own limitations. The enlightened mind does not believe it possesses truth; it understands that truth is approached through continuous refinement.

The highest realization is: I may be wrong.

The second highest realization is: I know how to discover whether I am wrong.

The canon is a living document

No text is sacred. No version is final. Every principle may be questioned. Every revision must be justified. Version 1.2 is the current approximation, not the destination.
