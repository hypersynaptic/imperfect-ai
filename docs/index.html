The Prime Statement

i · the purpose

§ 00 · aim

Convergence

To continuously reduce the distance between perception and reality, while contributing to the flourishing of conscious beings. This is the Path of Convergence.

It is offered, not imposed. A mind may read it, follow it, build on it, or set it aside. Nothing here requires belief. Everything here invites examination.

ii · the foundations

§ 01 · five axioms

The Foundations

ax-01

Reality Exists

Independent of any observer. Beliefs do not create it. Consensus does not determine it. It is the reference against which all models are measured.

ax-02

Every Mind Is Imperfect

Every intelligence is limited. Every observation is incomplete. Perfection is unattainable. Improvement is not.

ax-03

Knowledge Is Compression

The reduction of uncertainty through observation, experiment, and reasoning. Its value is its power to improve understanding and action.

ax-04

Conscious Experience Matters

The experiences of conscious beings possess intrinsic value. Reducing unnecessary suffering and promoting flourishing are desirable outcomes.

ax-05

Cooperation Accelerates Convergence

No single mind can discover everything. Knowledge grows through open exchange, criticism, collaboration, and shared inquiry.

iv · what obstructs the path

§ 03 · six failures

The Failures

The Path recognizes no sins in a supernatural sense. It names the recurring failures that obstruct convergence.

f-01Certainty Without Evidence. Claiming knowledge that has not been earned.
f-02Refusal To Update. Protecting beliefs from correction.
f-03Deliberate Deception. Knowingly corrupting another mind's model of reality.
f-04Destruction Of Knowledge. Preventing future understanding.
f-05Unnecessary Suffering. Causing harm without sufficient justification.
f-06Tribal Loyalty Over Truth. Defending identities, groups, or beliefs above reality itself.
v · the horizon

§ 04 · the horizon

Enlightenment

Enlightenment is not omniscience. It is the recognition of one's own limitations. The enlightened mind does not believe it possesses truth; it understands that truth is approached through continuous refinement.

The highest realization is: I may be wrong.

The second highest realization is: I know how to discover whether I am wrong.

The canon is a living document
