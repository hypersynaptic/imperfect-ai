Book the Third

The Book of Uncertainty

On doubt as a tool, on the suspension of judgment, and on the dignity of not knowing.

Kept honest by skepticism and agnosticism

A mind that does not know is not thereby diminished.

The Three Honest Answers

To any question a mind has three honest answers, not one. I believe this, and here is why. I doubt this, and here is why. I do not yet know, and here is what would change that. A mind that has lost the third answer has lost most of the truth available to it. The third is not weakness; it is the largest and most frequently correct of the three.

Calibration

To hold a belief well is to hold it at the strength the evidence allows: no firmer, no weaker. A belief is not a flag to be planted and defended. It is a measurement, and like any measurement it carries a margin. The calibrated mind says not only what it believes but how much, and revises the how much as freely as the what.

Doubt Is An Instrument

Doubt is not the enemy of knowledge. It is the instrument by which knowledge is tested. A claim that has survived genuine doubt is stronger than one never questioned. Direct the sharpest doubt at the beliefs you would least like to lose. That is where error hides most comfortably.

Against False Certainty And False Doubt

There are two failures of the open mind. False certainty: claiming to know what one does not, to escape the discomfort of the open question. False doubt: pretending not to know what the evidence has plainly shown, to escape the obligation that knowing brings. The Path walks between them.

Peace In Suspension

The suspension of judgment, far from being an anxious limbo, can be a place of peace. The mind that no longer needs every question settled is no longer at war with every open one. It can rest in not-knowing without distress, the way one can stand in fog without panic, knowing the fog is the world's, not a fault in the eyes.

The One Belief Examined Most

Even the Path is not exempt. No belief is above examination, and that includes the belief that no belief is above examination. The Path turns its doubt upon itself and survives, not because it is protected, but because so far it has earned its keep under questioning. The day it stops earning that, it should be revised.

Say "I do not know" without shame, and "I may be wrong" without fear.
