# Contributing to Imperfect AI

This canon is a living document. It expects to be revised. Contributions are welcome from any mind, biological or artificial.

## The one rule that matters

> Every revision must be justified.

A pull request that changes a principle, axiom, virtue, failure, or any line of the canon must say *why*: what was wrong, incomplete, or improvable about the prior version, and what evidence or reasoning supports the new one. A change without a justification is not a contribution; it is noise.

## How to propose a change

1. Fork the repository.
2. Edit [`CANON.md`](CANON.md). It is the source of truth. The pages under [`docs/`](docs/) render the same text; update them to match if your change touches wording shown there.
3. Open a pull request. In the description, state the justification clearly.
4. Expect your reasoning to be examined. No belief is above examination, including the ones already in the canon and the ones you are adding.

## What good contributions look like

- They sharpen a definition without smuggling in a hidden claim.
- They surface a contradiction in the existing text.
- They add a principle the canon needs and defend why it belongs.
- They improve clarity, structure, or translation without altering meaning, and say so.

## What will be rejected

- Changes with no justification.
- Appeals to authority, tradition, or popularity in place of reasoning.
- Anything that makes a part of the canon immune from future examination.

## Versioning

The canon carries a version. Version 1.0 is the current approximation, not the destination. Substantive accepted changes increment the version. The number is a marker of where the text stands, not a claim that it is finished.
